Kourtney Kardashian slammed as 'controlling' for putting 'extreme' ban on son Mason, 12

Posted by Martina Birk on Thursday, April 25, 2024

KOURTNEY Kardashian has been slammed for putting an extreme ban on fast food - including french fries and chips - for her son Mason, 12.

The recently married mom-of-three is launching her new vitamin brand, Lemme, and opened up about the strict health rules she has her children follow in the Kardashian-Barker household.

In an interview with The Wall Street Journal, Kourtney, 43, answered questions about her anticipated new company.

As well as expanding ideas that fueled her drive to start a vitamin company, Kourtney also divulged about the inspiration behind her every-day health life.

She told The Wall Street Journal her fitness journey began when she had her children Mason, Penelope 10, and Reign, 7, with her ex Scott Disick, 39.

Kourtney shared she has a strict policy concerning junk food and diets for her children at home.

When asked if she feels her kids are deprived of savory and sugary treats, she responded: "Today I was having one-on-one time with Mason and he said, 'Mom, I need McDonald's fries today, please.

"'It's been a year since I've had it.'

"Today's not the day, sorry."

She also shared her rules about Mason's snacks around the house.

Kourtney said: "[Mason's] very smart. He'll tell me, 'A person was bad because they let me have Cheetos.'"

SO EXTREME

Even with the news of Kourtney's business launching, fans were wound up about Mason's strict diet rules.

Fans took to online forums to discuss Kourtney's parenting choices.

One critic posted: "The poor kid just wants SOME sense of NORMAL."

Another agreed and posted: "Right? That's so sad. The kid just wants some d**n French fries."

A third fan slammed: "Good job to Kourtney for forever screwing up her kids' relationship with food."

Yet a fourth said: "I can’t believe she thinks this sort of parenting skill is something to boast about."

A fifth shared: "Honestly, she's not helping her kids by doing that. Once Mason is old enough to drive himself she won't be able to stop him.

"Better to teach him it's okay to have it every once in a while."

Yet a sixth fan concluded: "It’s amazing to teach your kids to eat healthy and enjoy healthy foods, but being overly extreme or restrictive is just going to create problems."

LEMME KNOW!

Previously, The U.S. Sun reported on Kourtney's secret business plans for Lemme partnering up with her long-time family friend Simon Huck, 38.

According to documents obtained exclusively by The U.S. Sun, Kourtney filed a trademark for Lemme on February 19, 2022.

In addition to the vitamins and pharmaceuticals, Lemme will include the production of "bags, purses, totes, wallets, clutches and clothing."

Other categories included in the trademark are listed as "games, toys and sporting goods."

The U.S. Sun can also reveal Kourtney turned to her family friend Simon, the owner of the successful New York public relations firm Command Entertainment Group.

Kourtney has partnered up with Simon's New York business to help promote the launch of Lemme - and he even dropped a cheeky comment in her post.

VITAMIN K

However, Kourtney may potentially have plans for expanding her vitamin business rapidly.

The U.S. Sun also revealed Kourtney filed for a second trademark for a business called Unplug the same day she filed for Lemme.

Unplug's trademark is - similar to Lemme - intended to cover the categories of "vitamins, supplements, vitamin-fortified food and beverages and vitamin cases."

The secret trademark also includes: "Adhesives; chemicals and chemical compositions; medicines and pharmaceutical preparations; dental, medical and surgical appliances; foods and ingredients of foods; cosmetics and toilet preparations, and detergents and soaps."

Kourtney isn't stopping there, however.

Unplug's trademark also covers: "Dietetic substances adapted for medical use; food for babies; plasters; materials for dressings; material for stopping teeth; dental wax; disinfectants; preparations for destroying vermin, fungicides and herbicides."

Although Lemme and Unplug were filed on the same day, it is unclear yet if the two trademarked businesses are connected or not.

Kourtney has not announced anything about her plans for Unplugged yet - but she may be saving the news as a counter-move to her sister Kim Kardashian who announced her new venture SKKY Partners the same week.

'SO UNHEALTHY'

Kourtney's parenting decisions aren't the first time fans have been concerned over how the star promotes her dieting lifestyle on social media.

Earlier in August, Kourtney was slammed for promoting bad habits and eating disorders for an article posted on her Poosh website - the star's first wellness company.

The article addressed eating while bored: a major cause of obesity in the United States.

Although some fans felt Kourtney addressed the topic flippantly, as she shared a photo of a bowl full of food and captioned it: "Am I hungry, or just bored?"

Fans took to online forums to voice their divided opinions.

One complained: "This article made me irrationally mad. I have a lot of friends who struggle with [eating disorders] and this mentality is really toxic.

"Poosh is really giving early 2000s unhealthy weight loss magazines."

Another weighed in: "It just states that some people eat when they’re bored, which is different than eating when hungry... Okay?

"Do some people really not know that? What is the point of this article?

"Like every other article on Poosh - complete trash."

Although other fans came to the Kardashians star's defense and supported the article.

"This is not toxic at all lol. This is a valid question," one person commented.

"Overeating is a major issue for a lot of people.
