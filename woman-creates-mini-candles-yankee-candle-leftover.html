Thrifty woman reveals how she melts down leftover Yankee Candle wax &amp; pours into cupcake cases to ma

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, July 8, 2024

CONFESSION time: as much as we love to bulk buy three-wick candles, we always end up throwing them away before they get to the bottom.

You know, that point when there's hardly any wax left and the flame starts burning the jar, leaving an ugly sooty stain on the side that's impossible to clean.

But rather than immediately lighting up the next one you have squirrelled away in your designated Yankee Candle cupboard (guilty as charged), one woman has revealed her genius hack for using up every last drop of wax.

Sharing her tip on the Facebook group Extreme Budget DIY & Life Hacks, the woman wrote: "When you have to bin your Yankee Candle because there's less than an inch left, [start by putting] the jar in a pan of boiling water.

"Then pour the melted wax into bun cases, once they have re-set you've got some melts for your burner!

"Making the most of every last bit of your candle. Thank me later."

And it wasn't just us who've had our minds blown by this genius trick - although others suggested only filling the jar with boiling water from the kettle.

One user replied: "That's such a fab tip!"

Another added: "I fill the jar with boiling water from the kettle, the left over wax melts and rises to the top then when the water goes cold the wax goes solid again and you can just snap it in half and pull it out."

Recommending another method of achieving the same result, a third replied: "I pour warm water into the jar as the wax will melt and float to the top to set then I just take it out when it goes cold and pop it in my burner."
