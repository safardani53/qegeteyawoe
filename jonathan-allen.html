Jonathan Allen’s recent brawl with Daron Payne 

Tensions began on the Washington Football Team after Jonathan Allen broke into a fight with Daron Payne. 

During Washington’s Sunday night loss to Dallas Cowboys, Allen took a full swing at his former Alabama teammate. 

The videos that have gone viral now show the two of them talking at first. Payne, then, jabbed a finger into the side of Allen’s head, who was unaware of what was going to happen. 

Following the attack, Allen was quick to swat Payne’s hand and took a full swing with his right, smacking Dallas player’s face. 

As expected, the fight took a nasty turn and soon caught the attention of many. 

However, the two were separated by three teammates with the help of coach Sam Mills Jr. 

After everything settled down, both players finished the game and ensured it was nothing but a brotherly disagreement. 

“Just a little brotherly disagreement; maybe the wrong place, wrong time, but it happened.”

Payne told ESPN, “You got brothers? You all fight, don’t you? S— happens.”

Nonetheless, things are going from bad to worse for Washington as they have lost three games in a row. And the brawl between two of their players is another fuss for them to answer.  

This mini-brawl can be considered frustrations boiling over after an inferior defensive series for the Washington team. 

But on the good side, the two are adamant about not letting this linger any further. 

“When something happens on the field, you never let it carry into the locker room. Things get heated, we fix them, we sit down as grown men and move on.” 

To this, Allen further added, 

“I’ll take full responsibility for my actions, and so will Payne.” 

What actually happened?

According to TV footages, Payne was seen getting into Allen’s face. Allen lost his cool after a long resistance of Payne poking his head. 

Allen, then, lost his cool and swung through Payne’s head before being separated by other members of the defensive line and the coach sitting nearby.

Jonathan Allen: Early Life & College 

On January 16, 1995, Allen was born in Anniston, Alabama (United States). He currently lives in Anniston, Alabama. 

Allen shifted to Pittsburgh when he was less than a year old. Before settling in Virginia, he also lived in other cities such as Seattle and North Carolina.

After Allen’s parents divorced, he stayed with his mother for about five years before moving in with his father.

The player has repeatedly stated that he wants to find his mother, whom he lost contact with since his third grade.

In Virginia, he began to play football at Stone Bridge high school, known for its stellar football team. It helped young Allen to furnish his skills in the game.

Likewise, he earned a spot as the Gatorade Football Player of the Year during his senior year. 

Allen wholeheartedly committed to the game of football during his college. After graduating from high school, he played college football for the University of Alabama. 

He was rated 5-star recruit and was also ranked top amongst his class. It was then that he got mass recognition. 

As a freshman at the University of Alabama in 2013, he played 13 games and made 16 tackles. Later, in 2015, he helped his team win the 2016 College Football Playoff National Championship.

Jonathan Allen: Professional Career

Jonathan Allen has had a successful career as a defensive tackle for the Washington Football team. But he had times when his stocks had dipped drastically.

Allen was projected to be one of the top five picks in the NFL draft when he came off his college at Alabama.

However, he was soon diagnosed with arthritis in one of his shoulders. He underwent surgery for both his shoulders. It created an unnecessary fuss within the teams. 

NFL analysts and experts predicted that Allen, even with a diagnosis of arthritis, would be a first-round pick.

Moreover, ESPN ranked him as the second-best defensive lineman, just behind Myles Garrett. And, he was ranked as the best defensive tackle by Sports Illustrated.

The 6’3″ giant was selected in the first round of the 2017 NFL draft itself by the Washington Redskin as the 17th overall pick. 

He then signed a four-year rookie contract on May 11, 2017, for a whopping sum of $11.59 million.

Allen suffered a Lisfranc injury while playing against the San Francisco 49ers. Later, he underwent surgery and was placed on an injured reserve in October 2017. 

Comeback 

After the plethora of injuries, Jonathan Allen returned for the 2018 season and soon starred in all 16 games.

He had 61 tackles and 15 quarterback hits, alongside eight sacks. 

Allen then switched over to defensive tackle after playing the defensive end position for the first three seasons of his career. 

Similarly, his starring performances got him the fifth-year option on his contract in April 2020. 

Allen signed a four-year contract extension on July 2021 for $72 million. Allen was even voted to the 2022 Pro Bowl in December 2021.
