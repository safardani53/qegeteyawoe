Coventry Hospital fire sees wards evacuated as smoke is seen billowing from building

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, August 26, 2024

A FIRE at Coventry Hospital has caused several wards to be evacuated as smoke was seen billowing from the building.

A dramatic video shows smoke pouring from an upper floor from the Walsgrave site of Coventry's University Hospital.

A number of wards in an outpatient building were evacuated as firefighters extinguished the blaze shortly after they arrived at the scene.

West Midlands Fire sent eight fire engines to douse the fire, which appears to have started in an extractor flue from a boiler room.

Firefighters carried out a phased evacuation and moved patients to safer areas of the hospital.

A spokesman for University Hospitals Coventry and Warwickshire Trust said: “We can confirm that this morning there has been a small fire in an outpatient building adjacent to the West Wing of University Hospital, Coventry.

“We have needed to move a small number of patients, who have been safely taken to other areas of the hospital.

"No further evacuations are needed.  There have not been any injuries.

 “We would like to thank West Midlands Fire Service, and also our staff who took quick action to respond to this incident.”
