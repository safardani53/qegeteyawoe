Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Friday, March 22, 2024

THE creators of a snack that quickly became a favorite among shoppers have responded to a fan's demand to bring it back, signaling a possible return to shelves.

One person who couldn't get enough of the savory treat recently took to social media to plead with the company that formerly made them.

The conversation began on Twitter courtesy of the UberFacts account, which has 13.5 million followers and often provides quick facts and questions for its audience.

"What discontinued food/beverage item do you wish would return with all your heart," UberFacts asked.

A reader quickly responded, advocating for General Mills' Peanut Butter Boppers.

"@GeneralMills bring them back!" the user pleaded while including an image of the product.

The food processing business then issued a reply that likely left several fans of the discontinued product hopeful for the future.

"We'll let our team know you're missing them!" the official General Mills account wrote.

According to the company's website, Peanut Butter Boppers launched in July 1985 and fell under the Nature Valley brand name.

They were also available in Fudge Chip, Honey Crisp, and Peanut Crunch flavors.

The snacks, which came in the shape of a small log, "featured a soft, creamy center made from real peanut butter wrapped in delicious crunchy coatings and sealed in a foil pouch for freshness," General Mills said.

Only 90 days after the product was introduced, it accounted for nine percent of the United States snack market.

It's unclear when the product was discontinued, but it occurred sometime before Betty Crocker released a recipe on its website for a copycat version of Peanut Butter Boppers.

Whether the item will make a comeback is uncertain, but General Mills has been made aware that it is missed, especially by children who grew up during the mid-1980s.

MORE DISCONTINUATIONS

Unfortunately, some other fan-favorite items from several retailers will remain off the shelves for the time being.

As The U.S. Sun previously reported, Aldi recently confirmed that a couple of sweet treats have been off the shelves since 2019.

A customer pleaded for the return of the store's knockoff of the Thin Mint Girl Scout Cookies.

However, the retailer established that the cookie treat would not be coming back.

"After looking into this more, we can confirm that these have been discontinued and are no longer available," Aldi said.

The same went for Benton's Homestyle Chocolate Chip Cookies.

Kroger also confirmed the discontinuation of its staple Brown Gravy Mix, which left customers furious.
