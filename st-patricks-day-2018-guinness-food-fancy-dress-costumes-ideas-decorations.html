St Patricks Day fancy dress ideas, decorations and food and drink

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Tuesday, June 18, 2024

ST Patrick's Day, which is a cultural and religious event that marks the traditional death date of Saint Patrick, is this Saturday.

Anyone with a drop of Irish blood in them celebrates the occasion - and here are some ideas to make the day extra fun.

 

Fancy dress

There's no celebration without novelty costumes, and plenty of shops offer special St Patrick's Day items to get you in the mood.

You can either go all out in a full on costume, or just opt for a funky bow tie or headgear.

Party delights are offering a full clover suit (£39.99) if you want to go for the ultimate "smart casual" look, and Joke are selling a hilarious rainbow costume  (£32.99)- but the pot of gold isn't included.

If you fancy something a little more low key, then there are plenty of trinkets you can accessorise with instead.

Poundland are selling a flashing bow tie, and Fun Fancy Dress have some shamrock sunglasses for just £2.30.

Otherwise,  you could choose a leprechaun hat for £2.99, or a glitter headband for £2.49, both from Party Delights.

Decorations

If you're not heading to an Irish bar this weekend, why not invite people over to your house?

Any party venue can be spruced up on a budget with cheap and cheerful items.

Hang up a green shamrock foil banner so that your guests know that they're in for a good time - Amazon have one for £6.99.

You could serve up your food on shamrock plates for the ultimate themed bash, and they cost just £1.74 from Party Packs.

Scattering your table with confetti is a great way to add some fun to your snacks, and Party Pieces are selling a pack for just 99p.

Or, go all out and get their table cloth - it also means that any spillages (after too much Guinness) won't stain your table or regular table cover.

Poundland are selling an inflatable shamrock, or you could even treat your guests to a fun pinata game, and Party Pieces are selling a fab one for £14.95.

Food and drink

It goes without saying that you'll definitely have to serve up some Guinness if you're hosting a St Patrick's Day party.

For snacks, an easy option would be to make simple cupcakes, and then cut some green icing in the shape of shamrocks to lay over them.

Or, make some biscuits and paint rainbows on them with some writing icing.

They would be easy enough for your child to have a go at, and you could even dab on some edible glitter for the 'pot of gold'.

Other rainbow themed ideas are a rainbow cake, or lots of different coloured jelly.

You could even bulk buy M&Ms or Skittles and have bowls of rainbow food to attract leprechauns.

Or for something more substantial, there are plenty of traditional Irish recipes available on the BBC website, such as beef stew and rarebit.

St Patrick's Day may cause inebriation after more than a few drinks. Stay safe
