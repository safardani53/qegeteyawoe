7 Best Sleep Masks 2022 | The Sun UK

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, April 17, 2024

SLEEP is so important for our mental and physical health, but a lot of us aren’t getting enough of it.

So we've rounded up some of the best sleep masks to help you get some much needed rest.

Do sleep masks really work?

The sleep chemical, melatonin, is produced when your brain senses complete darkness, which helps you sleep better and deeper.

But often, we don't sleep in total darkness.

Your curtains or blinds might not block out the light for example, or perhaps you have lots of gadgets in the room.

Or perhaps you work at night and need to nap during the day.

Whatever the reason, eye masks can be a shortcut to achieving total darkness - as long as they fit properly.

We've tested a small selection of eye masks and rounded up some alternatives below.

Unlike most of the eye masks we’ve featured, the Sensory Retreats Dreamy Eyes masks are single use.

Each gently scented mask comes individually wrapped, and as soon as you open it up, it starts to warm up.

We were also impressed by the fit, and loved how the scent wasn’t overwhelming at all.

We tested: This Is Silk eye mask

  • This Is Silk eye mask, £21.99 from This Is Silk - buy here

This oversized eye mask from This Is Silk is available in a range of colours and has been handmade using 22 momme silk.

Momme is basically the thread count used for silk and can range between 11 and 25.

The higher the count, the higher the density and the heavier it will be. Because of that, 22 momme is generally thought of as the perfect balance between weight and quality.

We thought this mask looked and felt lovely and luxurious and, because it came in a gift box, would make a lovely present for someone.

It's also available in a few different colours so it's easy to find a shade that suits you.

The oversized design meant that it completely blocked out the light without putting any pressure on our skin.

However, because the fabric is so soft and silky, the mask does slip off by the morning.

It’s not a big problem if your bedroom is dark or you’re a heavy sleeper but otherwise you may find yourself waking up before your alarm goes off in the morning.

The Echor Total Blackout eye mask is one of the bulkier designs we tested but it could be just right for those with false eyelashes or are having a nap during the day.

That said, it’s not one we would recommend to side sleepers - the foam padding means the eye mask is raised on all sides and you can definitely feel it pressing into your face when you’re lying on your side.

How often should I use eye masks?

You can use your eye mask as often as you need - just make sure they're washed regularly if you're using them every night.

Ideally, they should be cleaned at least weekly - see below.

Can you wash eye masks?

Anything that comes in regular contact with your skin needs to be washed frequently.

Just like you'd wash your pillowcases and clothes, you need to keep your eye mask germ free.

Most of the time you can machine wash your eye mask with the rest of your load, but double-check the washing instructions before you chuck it in.

Many sleep masks are made of silk, which you'll need to hand wash in cold water with a gentle soap and line dry.
