Mum claims she was left 'looking like Mick Hucknall' after blonde hair toner turned her locks ORANGE

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, September 28, 2024

A WOMAN has claimed that her ombre locks turned BRIGHT ORANGE after using a blonde toner from Superdrug.

After her mishap, NHS worker Steph Holland, 27, then penned a hilarious one-star review of the product, declaring: “woke up looking like Mick Hucknall, wouldn’t recommend”.

Steph, from Dukinfield, Greater Manchester, says she carefully applied the Jerome Russell BBlonde toner in White Gold to the ends of her hair last Friday night to counter the brassy tones.

But, after leaving the £4.49 product to develop for 40 minutes, horrified Steph says she was left with hair “like a fruit salad sweet” and desperate to cover it up.

Unsympathetic partner Adam Wilkes, 27, roared with laughter when he was shown Steph’s beauty blunder that night - but on Saturday morning hot-footed it to the shops to buy a cover-up dye.

Jerome Russell said they advise customers to carry out a strand test before using products and Superdrug said they were investigating the complaint.

Steph, said: “It was a disaster, I absolutely hated it.

“I was livid at the time but then I realised you have to have a bit of humour when these things go wrong.

“My hair’s usually brown with ombre blonde ends. It had started to go brassy at the bottom after I had it done two months ago so decided to use the toner to try and equalise it.

“I don’t have a shower at home so I did my hair in the bath.

“When I got out of the bath and looked in the mirror it had gone a reddy pinkish hue.

“I thought ‘I’ll rinse it and it’ll be fine’. I rinsed it and it wasn’t.

“I wasn’t happy, my hair looked like fruit salad sweets or fruit pastilles with that pinkish tinge."

The following morning, Adam went out to buy an ash brown dye kit in a bid to cover up Steph’s candy-coloured locks.

Steph added: “I refused to go out with my hair like that. I briefly considered going out with a hat on but a friend said people would still see the ends so decided against it.

“My hair’s naturally curly, when I saw it the following morning it reminded me of Annie and Mick Hucknall.”

Wanting to highlight what happened to her, Steph wrote a light-hearted review on the Superdrug’s site and Facebook page.

The one-star review reads: “Don’t buy this unless you’re auditioning for Annie.

“Hi there, after lengthy discussion with the Superdrug staff I bought this toner as I thought the colour was lovely and natural.

“It was great on applying and I thought I was going to look a million bucks… what I wasn’t expecting was to wake up looking like Mick Hucknall from Simply Red.

“Wouldn’t recommend – sorry.”

The mum of one picked up the toner from the Ashton-Under-Lyne branch of the store last Thursday after reading rave reviews online.

Steph said: “I’d read reviews online and it looked really nice.

“It says to leave it in between 25 to 40 mins, 45 minutes was the maximum, so I left it on for 40 minutes as I wanted it to take properly. I couldn't believe it when I looked in the mirror.

“The next day I put an ash brown dye on it and luckily it covered the pink. I suspect it will go lighter the more I wash it.

“I put the review on social media as I thought I might get a more immediate response."

Jerome Russell Sales and Marketing manager Gillian McClure said: “At Jerome Russell we want everyone who uses our products to have amazing hair.

“We are the UK’s number one blonding brand and have been for many years. Our range of products are sold in the millions, each year to very satisfied customers.

“We test our products thoroughly and all are compliant with European regulation 1223/2009.

“Our White Gold Toner is a beautiful on trend shade formulated for pre-lightened or naturally blonde hair.

“Colour results will vary depending on natural hair colour and can also be influenced by the condition of hair, any previous underlying colourant and porosity.

“We do advise our customers to carry out both a skin sensitivity test and strand test before using any of our products. The purpose of this is both for safety and also to make sure they get the amazing hair results they were looking for.”

A Superdrug spokesman said: “We are very sorry to hear about Ms Holland’s experience.

“We take customer complaints incredibly seriously and our customer service team has been in touch in order to gain additional information so we can liaise with Jerome Russell regarding her complaint.”
