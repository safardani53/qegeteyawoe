Posted by Aldo Pusey on Tuesday, April 9, 2024

SHE might have been getting older, but this queen of mature skin's makeup routine was making her look younger than ever before.

And her tips were already getting some eye-popping results - literally.

"This year I turned 51 and I really started tweaking my makeup to suit my more maturing features," said Kat James (@shinebykatjames).

She especially wanted to stay focused on this one part of the face: her eyes.

They are after all, the windows to the soul - and a more youthful appearance.

"I am doing my eye makeup completely different to make everything look more open," she explained.

The anti-aging expert removed all of her makeup over one eye in order to show the very noticeable differences.

Her first transformative tip included using an eye base from her favorite brand.

She carefully dabbed the $25 Mac pro longwear paint pot all over her lid.

The eye primer was described as providing a vibrant finish that lasted for 24 hours - and had gained hundreds of five-star reviews.

"I love that the color is buildable so it can be a light wash or bold and intense. You need at least one of these but I promise you'll be back for more!" wrote another enthused buyer.

Kat explained how concealer was also a viable alternative.

"So you are tapping this on your lid all the way from the lashes to the brow. That alone really opened up the lid," explained Kat.

She continued on with her makeover by mixing two shades in her eyeshadow palette - one warm and one cool color.

The $68 Makeup by Mario ethereal eyes eyeshadow palette which is constantly selling out, offers 12 shades in sueded matte, modern metallic, and glossy shimmer finishes.

And Kat wasn't the only one obsessed with the product.

"It's so easy to have the perfect eye from day to night. It's all just in this beautiful package. Perfect tones for blue eyes," wrote this pleased purchaser.

"Buttery texture, really good pigment. I love this palette I'm beyond impressed by it. I haven't put it in my kit yet, I want to enjoy it all to myself a little longer," said another happy customer.

She started in on her crease and explained what has made the most difference: "I add a lot of color on this inner corner here."

Kat also explained how adding shadow as the eyelid starts to droop helps to emphasize an extra sharp line.

Keeping up with Kat also involved using circular motions to create a pocket and then moving across the rest of her crease, ending by the eyebrow.

She gave a close up glimpse to see the pretty progress she was making.

Going in with a yogurt shade, she tapped the shadow on the inner corner to add some light and make everything pop.

Kat took the leftovers on her brush and added the light color to underneath her brow.

She also advised blending in any rough edges.

And the mature skin advisor wasn't done just yet as she added a darker shade onto an angled brush to use as a liner.

"I'm just going to do one little stamp here, like a wing, and then drag that across my crepey lid."

Then, with the angled brush pointing towards her nose, she stamped the inner part of her eyelid.

The beauty expert blended the rest of her crease color underneath her eye before applying some mascara in a pulling out motion.

Kat demonstrated how she moved the wand towards her eyebrow to achieve the ultimate cat eye look.

Exclaimed an elated Kat: "That's become my go-to look for a really open bright youthful eye. It just works."

And she wasn't the only one who noticed how her tricks were turning back the clock.

In fact, her look was so flawless it almost looked fake.
