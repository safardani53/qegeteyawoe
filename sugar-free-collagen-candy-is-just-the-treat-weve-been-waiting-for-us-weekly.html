What Is Collagen?
Collagen is a protein that binds tissue and makes up about 75% of the skin’s dry weight giving it volume to keep it looking plump and minimizing lines and wrinkles. However, as early as age 30, the body makes about 1% less collagen each year breaking it down faster than the body can replace it. Exposure to UV rays, cigarette smoke, pollution or having a poor diet can also speed up collagen loss.
