In Her Hands Is Set To Be Released On Netflix Soon

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Thursday, August 1, 2024

In Her Hands, a new documentary from Netflix is set to premiere on the streaming service on Wednesday, November 16, 2022, at 3 a.m. ET. The documentary follows Zarifa Ghafari, Afghanistan’s first female mayor, as she navigates many perils and hurdles in the midst of her country’s unstable political climate. Tamana Ayazi and Marcel Mettelsiefen directed the documentary. The film’s executive producers are Hillary Rodham Clinton and Chelsea Clinton.

Netflix’s In Her Hands will be an engrossing viewing.

On October 26, 2022, Netflix published the official trailer for In Her Hands, which shows Afghanistan’s chaotic political circumstances and Zarifa Ghafari’s battle. The trailer has a solemn tone that suits the subject matter well. In Her Hands’ teaser portrays quickly how Afghanistan’s stability was interrupted when the Taliban gained control of the nation in 2021. It depicts Ghafari’s frantic efforts to better the social position of women in her nation, including her battle for their education.

Along with the teaser, Netflix posted a short synopsis of the series on their official YouTube account, stating:

“In Her Hands” depicts the narrative of Zarifa Ghafari, one of Afghanistan’s first female mayors and the country’s youngest, at 26. The film follows her struggle to live and barely escape against the background of her country’s rapid collapse.”

Viewers should expect a fascinating and emotional documentary that highlights the struggle of a young lady battling for survival in a nation split apart by political views and beliefs, based on the trailer and description. It also delves into the deadly political upheaval that erupted in Afghanistan in August 2021. Viewers may anticipate a clear and detailed depiction of Zarifa Ghafari’s life, as well as the countless hardships and risks she has faced over the years. In Her Hands premiered at the Toronto Film Festival in September and got primarily mixed-to-positive reviews from reviewers, with praise focusing on its goals, tone, and nuanced representation of the issue.

More information about Zarifa Ghafari

Zarifa Ghafari was born in Kabul in 1994. Abdul Wasi Ghafari, an army officer, was reportedly killed by insurgents in November 2020. She apparently attended school in Afghanistan before moving on to further education in India. Ghafari was elected mayor of Maidan Shahr at the age of 24, becoming her the country’s youngest and first female mayor. Zarifa Ghafari faced several perils and obstacles as the country’s mayor. She apparently faced numerous assassination attempts and got countless death threats.

Ghafari is noted for her struggle against women in the nation, as she attempted to assist them to obtain access to higher education throughout the country’s political upheaval. Ghafari is said to have been forced to escape her homeland when the Taliban gained control of Afghanistan in August 2021. She is presently living in Germany.
