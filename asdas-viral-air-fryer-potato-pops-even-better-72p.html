Foodie shares how to make Asda's viral Air Fryer potato pops even better - and they cost just 72p

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, January 1, 0001

YOU’VE probably already seen them on social media, but now Asda has revealed how to make their viral potato pops even better.

The frozen potato snack, which cost just 72p as part of the supermarket’s Just Essentials range, has gone down a storm with TikTok users who said they taste amazing when cooked in their Air Fryer. 

After seeing everyone go wild for the easy to make, but tasty dish, Asda decided to get in on the action and show their customers how they can give them a further upgrade.

They posted a video to their TikTok account, which can be found at @asda, to let their 71.3k followers in on the culinary secret. 

Asda uploaded a recipe for their “TikTok trending Air Fryer Salt and Pepper Potato Pops.” 

They added in the caption: “We’re not drooling, you’re drooling.

“The ultimate fakeaway right here. Are you going to be making these?”

The supermarket then lifted the lid on how to make them, plus the ingredients you’ll need along the way. 

Alongside the step-by-step tutorial, a voiceover said: “Have you seen these Asda potato pops all over TikTok?

“Well, if you want to do them too, here’s a great way to fakeaway.

“First off, put the Essentials potato pops in the Air Fryer for about 8 minutes.

“Next up, dice pepper, chillies, an onion and, lastly, some garlic.

“Then heat up some sesame seed oil in a pan and add the veg.”

Once the veg is cooking nicely, it’s time to turn your attention to the salt and pepper part of the recipe.

The foodie from Asda continued: “Next up, we’re making the salt and pepper mix.

“Some salt, chinese 5 spice, some grounded chilli flakes, garlic granules, ground ginger and lastly some ground white pepper.

“Add that into your pan with some soy sauce and give it a good mix through.

“Then, when your potato pops are nice and crispy, make sure they’re nice and covered.”

The final stage – and the dish’s secret ingredient – is Asda’s chip shop curry sauce mix, which costs £1 for 160g.

Asda added: “Just add water and stir it up.”

Once this is poured over the potatoes and veg, it’s time for the best part – tucking in to see how it tastes.

Asda concluded: “So there we go, super easy salt and pepper potato pops. Just look at that steamy, crispy goodness.”
